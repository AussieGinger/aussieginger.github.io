Chocolate Wattled Bat

Chalinolobus Morio

Research Results

Chocolate Wattled Bats were observed in moderate numbers on the Victorian Bio-Diversity Atlas (A total of 11 were observed).

They were observed in a number of habitats: Lake Bed Vegitation, Black Box Vegitation, River Red Gum Forest Vegitation, Chenopod (Saltbush) Shrubland. As well as a combination of these vegitations.

Bat Information

The Chocolate Wattled Bat was also observed in the area in an earlier survey conducted in 1994.

Chocolat wattled bats roos in hollows of old trees mostly, however they also roost in disused birds' nests. They have also been observed roosting in/on structures (Houses ect.). As well as caves in the nullabor region.

The Chocolate Wattled Bat feeds on small moths and insects

Chocolate Wattled Bats are vulnerable to loss of roost sites in tree hollows as well as feeding grounds due to forestry activities. Clearing for agriculture and housing also pose a risk.
