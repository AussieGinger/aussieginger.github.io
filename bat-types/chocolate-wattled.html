Chocolate Wattled Bat


Chalinolobus Morio

Research Results

Chocolate Wattled Bats were observed in moderate numbers on the Victorian Bio-Diversity Atlas (A total of 11 were observed).
They were observed in a number of habitats: Lake Bed Vegitation, Black Box Vegitation, River Red Gum Forest Vegitation and Chenopod (Saltbush) Shrubland. As well as a combination of these vegitations.
The Chocolate Wattled Bat was also observed in the area in an earlier survey conducted in 1994.


Bat Information

  • Chocolate wattled bats roosts in hollows of old trees mostly, however they also roost in disused birds' nests and have been observed roosting in/on structures (Houses ect.). As well as caves in the nullabor region.
  • They roost in colonies of 20 - Hundreds of bats.
  • The Chocolate Wattled Bat feeds on small moths and insects
  • Chocolate Wattled Bats are vulnerable to loss of roost sites in tree hollows as well as feeding grounds due to forestry activities. Clearing for agriculture and housing also pose a risk.
