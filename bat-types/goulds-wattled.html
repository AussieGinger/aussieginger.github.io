Gould's Wattled Bat

Chalinolobus Gouldii

Research Results

Gould's Wattled Bats were observed in high numbers on the Victorian Bio-Diversity Atlas (A total of 38 were observed).

They were observed in a number of habitats: Black Box Vegitation, Lignum Vegitation, River Red Gum Forest Vegitation and Chenopod (Saltbush) Shrubland. As well as a combination of these vegitations.

The Gould's Wattled Bat was also observed in the area in an earlier survey conducted in 1994.

Bat Information

Gould's wattled bats roosts in hollows of old trees mostly, they also roost in ceilings or basements of buildings. They have also been observed roosting in lighter shrubery. A typical colony size is around 30 bats.

The Gould's Wattled Bat feeds on a variety of insects. This can include: Scarab Beetles, Caterpillars, Crickets as well as Moths. This depends on the time of year.

Gould's Wattled Bats are vulnerable to loss of roost sites in tree hollows as well as feeding grounds due to forestry activities. Clearing for agriculture and housing also pose a risk.
