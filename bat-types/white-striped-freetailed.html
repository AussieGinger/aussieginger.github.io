White Striped Long Eared Bat

Austronomus Australis

Research Results

White Striped Freetailed Bat were observed in the lowest numbers on the Victorian Bio-Diversity Atlas (A total of 1 was observed).

They were observed in only Lake Bed Vegitation

Bat Information

  • The White Striped Freetailed Bat was not observed in the area in the earlier survey conducted in 1994.
  • White Striped Freetailed bats roos in hollows of old trees mostly, however they also roost under loose bark.
  • The White Striped Freetailed Bat feeds on small insects flying above the canopy.
  • White Striped Freetailed Bats are vulnerable to loss of roost sites in tree hollows as well as feeding grounds due to forestry activities. Clearing for agriculture and housing also pose a risk.

Fun Fact!

The White Striped Freetailed bat is the largest of the freetailed bats and one of the only microbats that a call humans can hear.
